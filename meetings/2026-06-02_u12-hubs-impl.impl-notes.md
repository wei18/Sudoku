## Decisions during implementation

### D1. `HubLoadState` constraint — `Hashable & Sendable` not `Identifiable & Equatable`

Spec §3.1 wrote `Item: Sendable & Equatable & Identifiable`. But:
- Leader's task statement says `enum HubLoadState<Item: Hashable & Sendable>`.
- `ForEach` over a homogeneous array doesn't strictly need `Identifiable` — it accepts `(id:)`. But Sudoku's `DailyCard` IS `Identifiable` so we can rely on it.
- Decision: follow Leader's looser constraint on the enum (`Hashable & Sendable`), and require `Identifiable` on the shell view's `Item` since we use `ForEach(items)`. The enum stays cheaper to declare for consumers that don't need ForEach.

Actually re-reading — `Hashable` does NOT imply `Identifiable`. To keep ForEach working without `id:` we need `Identifiable`. Two ways:
- (a) Constrain enum to `Hashable & Sendable`, shell view tightens to `Hashable & Sendable & Identifiable`.
- (b) Use `ForEach(items, id: \.self)` in the shell.

Picked (a) — `Identifiable` on the shell view's where-clause. The enum is generic over a looser bound; the rendering shell adds the `Identifiable` requirement at use site. This matches Leader's spec for the enum and the proposal's spec for the view.

### D2. `Equatable` conformance on HubLoadState

Proposal §3.1 writes `Sendable, Equatable`. Leader says `(Sendable, Equatable where Item: Equatable)`. The array case requires Item: Equatable. Since `Hashable: Equatable`, Item is always Equatable for the enum's bound. So unconditional `Equatable` works.
